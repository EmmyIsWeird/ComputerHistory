The Inventor of Computer Punch Cards

Who invented the Computer Punch Card?

It was first invented by Herman Hollerith. The New York City Board of Health first
tested using them for vital statistics tabulation. After being successfully tested,
computer punch cards were adopted for use in the 1890 census.

Where did his idea for the invention come from?

His idea of punching holes in a medium to store information did not just
pop into his head randomly one day. The idea came from seeing punched cards used
to control Jacquard looms. In France, 1810, Jacquard originated the idea of using
a punched card for controlling weaving looms.
