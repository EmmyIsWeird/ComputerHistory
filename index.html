Punch cards were used back when the first computers were made.
They were used for storing data for the computers.



What were they used for? How did they "store" data?

Punch cards were used for to store data and information for people and the computers.
The punch card "stored" data when it had hole punched into the paper card. The holes would
be put in certain order to represent certain data.

Computer Punch Cards were very useful at the time. They did not store very much data.
Since they could not store much data, the punch cards were made by the millions. If you had a stack
of punch cards with data in order, you would have to be very careful to keep them in order.
If they fell you wouldn't know the order. You would have to make new ones.







Herman Hollerith's Life

Herman Hollerith was born in Buffalo, New York, U.S. Februrary 29, 1860. He took his education at the
Columbia University School of Mines. He graduated from the school in 1879. Immediately after graduating,
Mr. Hollerith became an assistent teacher. He worked alongside his teacher William P. Trowbridge. He briefly
taught at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. In 1890 he had invented machines to record statistics
by reading punched cards of information.

The invention of Computer Punch Cards was a success in the U.S. It was
an even bigger hit in Europe where it was widely adopted for a large amount of statistic purposes. In the year
1896 Herman organized an offical company. It was called the Tabulating Machine Company. It was rooted in New York.
It eventually grew larger and became the International Buisness machines Corporation (IBM).

Herman married on September 15, 1890. He married Beverley Talcott. They had six child named Virginia,
Richard, Charles, Nannie, Lucia, Herman Jr. Sadly Herman Hollerith died from a heart attack, in Washington
D.C. He passed away on November 17, 1929 at the age of 69.

The Invention of Computer Punch Cards

Who invented the Computer Punch Card?

It was first invented by Herman Hollerith. The New York City Board of Health first
tested using them for vital statistics tabulation. After being successfully tested,
computer punch cards were adopted for use in the 1890 census.

Where did his idea for the invention come from?

His idea of punching holes in a medium to store information did not just
pop into his head randomly one day. The idea came from seeing punched cards used
to control Jacquard looms. In France, 1810, Jacquard originated the idea of using
a punched card for controlling weaving looms.
